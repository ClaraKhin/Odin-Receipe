Dragon's Beard Candy

Dragon's Beard Candy, originating from ancient China's Han Dynasty, is a confectionary marvel.
The candy got its name from the process of stretching a rice flour and sugar dough into thin,
sticky strands resembling a dragon's beard. Legend has it that an Imperial Court chef impressed
the Emperor with this technique, leading to the birth of Dragon's Beard Candy.

Ingredients

  • 1000g / 2.2lb of sugar
  • 100g / 3.5oz of light corn syrup or maltose
  • 1 tsp of white vinegar
  • 500ml or 2 cups of water
  • 3 to 4 cups of corn starch or rice flour
  • 2 to 3 cups of peanuts

Direction

  1. Creating the syrup is as simple as combining the sugar, corn syrup, vinegar, and water in a saucepan. No fuss, just a quick dump and mix!
  2. Securely attach the candy thermometer to the edge of the saucepan, ready to monitor the temperature with ease.
  3. Apply medium-high heat and aim for a temperature of 133°C (271°F). Cook for 20-25 minutes, ensuring an optimal result.
  4. Resist stirring the syrup to prevent the formation of sugar crystals caused by agitation.
  5. Cool the syrup to 100°C (212°F) before pouring it into a plastic mold. For silicone baking molds, no need to wait—pour away!
  6. While waiting for the syrup to cool or while it boils to the desired temperature, dry roast cornstarch or rice flour in a pan for 10 minutes over low to medium heat, ensuring it doesn't brown. Set it aside to cool alongside the syrup pucks.
  7. Next is to dry roast the peanuts in the same pan until cooked. Once that is done just chuck it into a food processor, blender or even chop it with a knife.
  8. Coat the ring with cornstarch, shape the candy with your hands, and dip as needed to prevent sticking in humid conditions.
  9. Stretch the ring evenly, coat with cornstarch, and increase its size. Shape into a figure-8, fold for two strands, and coat with cornstarch again.
  10. Work the strands into figure-8 shapes, fold for more strands, and dip in cornstarch. Repeat this process for 11 folds, yielding around 2048 strands. Optionally, continue for 3 more folds to reach a total of 14 pulls and achieve 16384 strands.
  11. Gently tear a 4-inch section of the "beard" to create a space for filling. Avoid cutting it, as it may cause merging, which should be avoided.
  12. Just spoon in some chopped peanuts in the middle of the 4inch bit, fold it in half and then roll it up. Done!
